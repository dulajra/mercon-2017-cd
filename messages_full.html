Prof. Nalin Wickramarachchi

Department of Electrical Engineering
University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka

I am pleased to welcome you to Moratuwa Engineering Research Conference 2017. We feel greatly honoured to be able to host this International Multidisciplinary Engineering Research Conference for the 3rd consecutive time in 2017 at the University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka. In addition to the main two day conference program, several keynote addresses and a series of workshops have been arranged to enhance the educational value of the event.

MERCon was included in SCOPUS last two years and it was considered as a big step towards its international credibility. This time around, the conference has indeed proved that to be the case by attracting high quality papers from 18 countries other than Sri Lanka, representing all regions in the world. Out of a total of 539 authors who submitted 183 papers for evaluation, the breakdown is 24% international and 76% local authorships. Among the 91 accepted papers, 15 international countries are represented leaving the international to local authorship ratio unchanged. The acceptance ratio was about 50%. Congratulations to the organisers who could be proud of this achievement in such a short while!

Success of a research conference like the MERCon is eventually measured by its acceptance by the peers as a reliable and useful source of high quality research output. Acceptance by peers is in turn measured nowadays by its citation counts such as h-index. I am confident that MERCon will scale this hurdle too in not so distant future.

Let me wind up by expressing my sincere thanks to all the authors, reviewers, sponsors and MERCon committee members who contributed to make MERCon 2017 a success. I sincerely hope that you will greatly benefit from the workshops, keynote addresses and technical presentations organised for you during these three days.

Dr. H.M.N. Dilum Bandara

Department of Computer Science and Engineering
University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka

On behalf of the Program Committee, it is with great pleasure that I welcome you to the MERCon 2017 technical program at the Civil Engineering Complex of the University of Moratuwa.

The program includes 91 papers selected from 183 submissions. The Program Committee (PC) of 43 distinguished experts used a single-blind review process. A three-member panel reviewed each paper, where each panel consisted of at least one reputed international researcher. Moreover, all papers underwent a similarity check with the help of the EDAS conference management system. When applicable authors were given an opportunity to justify their similarity score, and papers with satisfactory justifications (as determined by the PC) were considered for further processing. Only two papers did not receive a satisfactory justification. The PC meet once a week for the last four months and almost every day within the last two weeks. Paper acceptance/rejection decisions were based on reviewer comments, and weighted average of paper ranking and reviewer confidence level. All reviews and additional comments from the PC meeting were released to the authors. We hope that this information will help all authors improve their research, regardless of the outcome for their submission. Camera-ready papers were further scrutinized for addressing the reviewer comments and template compliance.

The conference includes 15 sessions on Big Data, Machine Learning, and Cloud Computing; Biomedical Engineering and Instumentation; Chemical and Process Engineering; Civil and Environmental Engineering; Energy and Environmental Engineering; Engineering Mathematics & Statistics; Machine Vision and Image Processing; Materials Science and Engineering; Mechanical Engineering Systems; Micro/Nano Electro Mechanical Systems, Mechatronics, and Micromechatronics; Power Systems, Electrical Machines and High Voltage Engineering; Robotics and Control; Signal Processing and Telecommunication Systems; Software Engineering; Technology Management; Textile and Apparel; and Transport and Logistics Management. In addition to the paper presentations, the program includes the poster session of the “ERU Symposium”. We are also particularly excited about the four keynote talks. The first keynote by Prof. Dr.-Ing. Christoph Bayer of the Technical University of Applied Sciences Nürnberg, Germany discusses about “Process Design and Information Systems”. Prof. Srikantha Herath of the United Nations University, Japan talk about initiatives taken to manage Urban Water in Metro Colombo. Two plenary talks by Dr. Darshana Muthumuni of the Manitoba HVDC Research Centre, Canada and Dr. Susumu Sugiyama of the Ritsumeikan University, Japan focus on more technical discussions on “Power Outages and Large-Scale Renewable Energy Penetration ” and “Environmental Friendly Polymer MEMS Technology ”, respectively. MERCon further hosts six workshops on contemporary topics such as Integrating Renewable Energy Resources: PSCAD-Based Network Integration Studies (by Dr. Darshana Muthumuni, Manitoba HVDC Research Centre, Canada); Waste Water Treatment Fundamentals, Plant Monitoring, and Operations (by Dr. P.G. Rathnasiri, University of Moratuwa); Modeling and Simulation of MEMS/NEMS Based Smart Devices and Systems (by Dr. Y.W.R. Amarasinghe, University of Moratuwa); Photovoltaic Power Conversion Systems: Current Trends (by Dr. Dulika Nayanasiri, University of Moratuwa); Modelling Wireless Sensor Networks with Network Simulator 3 (by Dr. Rajiv Pandey, Amity University Lucknow Campus, India); and Writing, Publishing, Presenting, and LaTeX (by Dr. Ranga Rodrigo, University of Moratuwa). We hope that all technical sessions, poster session, keynotes, and workshops will enable attendees to get exposure to the whole program and delve into the detailed contributions of the papers with their authors during the conference.

The MERCon 2017 program is the result of the hard work of the PC and TPC members, IEEE student branch of the University of Moratuwa, Postgraduate students, as well as ERU and University support staff that sacrificed a significant portion of their busy academic and professional time to provide service to our university and the research community. I am grateful for their professionalism in all aspects of their duties. I would also like to thank the external reviewers for their thorough reviews, often at a very short notice. I am particularly thankful to the PC members that diligently shepherded papers, working closely with the authors. I would also like to recognize all volunteered to be session chairs during the conference. Moreover, I would also like to recognize the co-sponsorships by the IEEE, IEEE Sri Lanka Section, Sri Lanka Section chapter of IEEE Power and Energy Society, Sri Lanka Section chapter of IEEE Robotics and Automation Society, and Faculty of Graduate Studies of the University of Moratuwa. Furthermore, I would like to recognize the support and encouragement from the Vice Chancellor, Deputy Vice Chancellor, Dean Engineering, Heads of Departments, Directors of Divisions, and Faculty members of the University of Moratuwa. Finally yet importantly, I would like to thank all the authors that submitted papers to MERCon 2017. The strong program is a reflection of a thriving research community with hard working members with broad interests.

I hope you all enjoy the technical program of MERCon 2017 and the opportunity to exchange ideas and interact with colleagues as much as I have enjoyed being part of delivering it.
