MESSAGES

General Chair
Prof. W.P.S. Dias
University of Moratuwa
Sri Lanka

Since last year (2015) MERCon publications are visible in the SCOPUS database/index. This is a significant achievement because SCOPUS is increasingly being used by many agencies to judge the research performance of both individuals and institutions. Both the THES (Times Higher Education Supplement) and QS (Quacquarelli Symonds) university ranking systems use SCOPUS. For this reason the University of Moratuwa is moving towards treating SCOPUS as a benchmark for quality publications. So MERCon’s inclusion in SCOPUS is indeed a big step towards its international credibility; it will also probably attract progressively better quality papers in years to come, including overseas ones.

Research metrics are now being used worldwide to reward research performance, and we must all be aware of such moves. Even in Sri Lanka, we could see research allowances being based on metrics such as the h-index in time to come. Google Scholar is the most popular database/index and most of us may be aware of our h-indices on Google Scholar. We will probably find that our corresponding h-indices on SCOPUS are somewhat lower. This is because SCOPUS is more discriminating in its selection of sources for inclusion. Younger researchers with long careers in front of them must take cognizance of such realities.

At the end of the day however, the quality of research will improve not primarily through researchers being monitored via metrics, but rather through the internal motivation of researchers themselves who work to self set standards in order to achieve excellence. Excellence however, is not easy to find or define, as Socrates’ friend, Meno, discovered...

“And how are you going to search for this [i.e. excellence], Socrates, when you don’t have the faintest idea what it is? Which of the things that you don’t know will you suppose that it is, when you are searching for it? And even if you do come across it, how are you going to know that this is the thing you didn’t know?”

Program Chair
Dr. H.M.N. Dilum Bandara
University of Moratuwa
Sri Lanka

On behalf of the Program Committee, it is with great pleasure that I welcome you to the MERCon 2016 technical program at the Civil Engineering Complex of the University of Moratuwa.

The program includes 76 papers selected from 155 submissions. The Program Committee (PC) of 31 distinguished experts used a single-blind review process. A three-member panel reviewed each paper, where each panel consisted of at least one reputed international researcher. Moreover, all papers underwent a similarity check with the help of TurnItIn facility provided by the University Library. When applicable authors were given an opportunity to justify their similarity score, and papers with satisfactory justifications (as determined by the PC) were considered for further processing. Only two papers did not receive a satisfactory justification. The PC meet once a week for the last four months and every day within the last two weeks. Paper acceptance/rejection decisions were based on reviewer comments, and weighted average of paper ranking and reviewer confidence level. All reviews and additional comments from the PC meeting were released to the authors. We hope that this information will help all authors improve their research, regardless of the outcome for their submission.

The conference includes 12 sessions on Big Data and Software Engineering; Biomedical Engineering and Soft Computing; Micro/Nano Electro Mechanical Systems, Mechatronics, and Micromechatronics; Telecommunication Systems; Mechanical Engineering Systems; Civil and Environmental Engineering; Transport, Logistics and Technology Management; Materials Science and Engineering; Power Systems, Electrical Machines and High Voltage Engineering; Chemical and Process Engineering; Machine vision and Image Processing; Robotics and Control. In addition to the paper presentations, the program includes the poster session of the “ERU Symposium”. We are also particularly excited about the four keynote talks. The first keynote by Prof. Monte Cassim of the Ritsumeikan Asia Pacific University, Japan discusses about the possibility of “Regaining Sri Lanka Through Science and Technology Innovation - Engineering Research and the J-SLIP Initiative”. Prof. Saman K. Halgamuge of the University of Melbourne, Australia talk the all possibilities when Data Engineering Meets Health. Two plenary talks by Prof. Karu Esselle of the Macquarie University, Australia and Prof. Jagath K. Samarabandu of the Western University of Canada focus on more technical discussions on “Engineering Radio Waves for Future Wireless Communication and Biomedical Systems” and “Dynamic Network Security- Intrusion Detection and Response”, respectively. We hope that all technical sessions, poster session, and keynotes will enable attendees to get exposure to the whole program and delve into the detailed contributions of the papers with their authors during the conference.

The MERCon 2016 program is the result of the hard work of the PC members, IEEE student branch of the University of Moratuwa, Postgraduate students, as well as ERU and University support staff that sacrificed a significant portion of their busy academic and professional time to provide service to our university and the research community. I am grateful for their professionalism in all aspects of their duties. I would also like to thank the external reviewers for their thorough reviews, often at a very short notice. I am particularly thankful to the PC members that diligently shepherded papers, working closely with the authors. I would also like to recognize all volunteered to be session chairs during the conference. Moreover, I would also like to recognize the co-sponsorships by the IEEE, IEEE Sri Lanka Section, Sri Lanka Section chapter of IEEE Power and Energy Society, Sri Lanka Section chapter of IEEE Robotics and Automation Society, the National Science Foundation, and University Grants Commission - Sri Lanka. Furthermore, I would like to recognize the support and encouragement from the Vice Chancellor, Dean Engineering, Heads of Departments, Directors of Divisions, and Faculty members of the University of Moratuwa. Finally yet importantly, I would like to thank all the authors that submitted papers to MERCon 2016. The strong program is a reflection of a thriving research community with hardworking members with broad interests.

I hope you all enjoy the technical program of MERCon 2016 and the opportunity to exchange ideas and interact with colleagues as much as I have enjoyed being part of delivering it.
